PROJECT DESCRIPTION

OVERVIEW
  • Topic:

    • Our group project is about how a game can act as an effective learning environment by integrating reflection into the process of play while studying in lecture or tutorial class. The game here resembles a legendary Mario game, however, the extraordinary here is the unique playstyle, a character in game that has to answer the question as soon as possible to get a higher mark and pass to another high level . The player uses the directional arrows on the keyboard to negotiate the direction of character to jump or to go left and right.

    The point element in the game could help students stay focused on their studying and increase the competitiveness among students in class to optimize the efficiency in the field of education.

  • Motivation:
    • All of us chose this idea because we wanted to experience and learn how to build a game by ourselves. One study of the popular multiple-choice quiz game Kahoot found that it improved students’ attitudes toward learning and boosted their academic scores. Most games are about entertaining and valuable awards that make individuals have a sense of achievement, however we want to create a brand new value in games that are educational. We do believe that our project is interesting because it can be applied in reality and brings huge efficiency. This will be in fit with current IT trends, because people's demand for entertainment in the midst of the Covid-19 outbreak is higher than ever. Furthermore, a future manager should be able to see that we are tech enthusiasts that are always up to date on the IT industry and have game development experience as a result of this.
  • Landscape:
    • Among countless entertainment games, one stood out the most and they are Nintendo. Nintendo is a global Japanese video game corporation headquartered in Kyoto, Japan. They are well-known for creating video games and game consoles. However, they mostly focus on the game for amusement purposes, whereas our venture aims to transcend beyond this. We decided to include some interesting features in our product, such as a quiz and a shopping cart. Students may engage more in class with quizzes, while the store features with accumulated coins generate a sense of success in players, keeping them in the game as long as feasible.

Aim

While all members of our group have a huge interest in the game industry, we decided to create a game by ourselves. However, due to the members lacking experience and knowledge about programming, the aim of this project is to create a basic platform game. A platform game or platformer is a style of game where the player has to control the character moving through obstacles in the given environment to complete the level.

First of all, our game is programmed using pygame, which is a suite of cross-platform Python modules for creating video games. It offers sound and graphics libraries that may be utilized with the Python programming language. There are a range of basic features that the game needs to follow:

  1. Game world: An environment for the character to move around
  2. Game main character: A figure that player can control to play this game
  3. Obstacles: Block the player in some locations
  4. Enemies: Locate in specific locations in each map, they try to attack when encounter the player
  5. Stuff to collect: Bonus point if the player collect on the way
  6. Quests to complete: tasks that required player to complete in order to gain rewards

Our first priority is to make the game run smoothly with those functions as mentioned above. Besides that, we are developing extra levels for multiple difficulties and various designs for the map as well.

Moreover, we also design a website to present our project with a better view and easier access to each part.

Scope and Limits
Several hours were spent deliberating among the members of our team before agreeing on a three-phase scope, with each phase having its own set of objectives and limits. During this first phase, we're primarily concerned with building the framework for the breadth of our many activities. The goal of this phase is to finish all the background work, which includes writing portions of the report and putting together the essential structure for our games. The second phase of our endeavor included developing a version of our game that was oriented toward people. Many things must be done for this to be successful. In the first instance, we could create a platform that would allow individuals to log in using their own accounts. Second, creating a database to store information about the stages the user has completed as well as coins and bonuses earned while playing the game, which includes establishing a technique and creating at least a few game levels. The scope of the project has been expanded in the third phase to include the creation of a working game that may potentially be sold or at the very least used as a proof of concept for a future commercial enterprise. Therefore, substantial polishing, the acquisition of all essential assets, the implementation of all marketing activities, and the completion of the game itself will be required. As soon as the game is released, a "stretch goal" is considered depending on the number of copies sold, and maybe more events, skins, and incentives for players are added to the game's roster.

Risks
It is hard to avoid any risk when starting up and working towards the end of a project. These are 2 risks that we identify and speculate regarding our project:
  • - Risk 1: Financial:
    • Like most forms of software development, the team will make no revenue from the duration of the work itself. Indeed, there are some startup pages such as Kickstarter, but it comes with many risks. It could be that the demo may not reach many people or not many feels interested in it yet. Hence, the ground funding campaign may not be enough to support members during the time. Unlike a lot of other forms, game developers usually make game without guaranteed customer even when the product is completed. The team will usually suffer financial loss during the producing time. However, if the project takes too much time or if the sales forecast was misread or wrong, the team will face even worse loss. As a small team, we will be burdened by time pressure and economic cost during the period of producing this project. We speculated them so we chose to have an adequate scope for this project in order to minimize this risk, but this does not fully eliminate it. If the expenses required to walk through this project to the end are above average starting capital, even if it is a passion project, the opportunity cost could result in the team dissolution.
  • - Risk 2: No demand:
    • As mentioned above, one of the most dangerous risks for a video game is when there is not enough buyer leading to minimum or even no sales. Each project relies on forecasting tools to determine how success the project will be. Demand risk refers to the forecast might not give the accurate prediction. No matter the reason, in an industry built around pushing demand, a project will sink and can lead to studio closure if the scope was too big. While our project is small, we do not have the same marketing power like an entire industry because the project will be constructed ourselves with a fluctuated financial and is mostly fueled more by passion than reason. The fear of no demand towards our project is realistically apprehensive. However, the project had been intended as more of a learning experience rather than creating a product that is globally viral. Although we may experience damage in finance lost, we still manage to learn from the work we have done and bring entertainment to customers even in just a short time.

Game developer

You need to have great design expertise, in-depth knowledge of coding software, and the ability to work as part of a team to succeed as a video game developer. A top-tier video game developer efficiently transforms the design team's concepts into a virtual world that can be resized and adjusted to match the requirements of the game. We require a candidate with several years of experience working in a team for this position so that he or she can engage and train newer developers or interns in the team. Moreover, constant updating and self-learning about new trends in the game business is required. This role is crucial to the project's success due to the game's sophistication.

Responsibilities:

  • Converting design concepts into functional game programming.
  • Creating the game's underlying engine.
  • Contributing to sound and graphics design.
  • Designing character animation and levels.
  • Setting game checkpoints and milestones.
  • Debugging and troubleshooting program.

Requirements:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Software Engineering or Information Technology or Computer Science.
  • Experiential knowledge of the entire game design process.
  • Expertise in game programming languages includes C, C++, and Java.
  • Extensive experience building libraries and APIs is an advantage.
  • Deep understanding of current gaming trends.
  • Capacity to analyze and handle complicated software and technical challenges.

Game designer

We're recruiting for a game designer that embraces all types of games, has a passion for coding and storytelling, and has a decent understanding of the industry, market, and target customers. Game designers are expected to lead teams of programmers, illustrators, animators, and music producers, as well as manage deadlines and expenses.

Responsibilities:

  • Delivering innovative gaming concepts to clients and executives.
  • Keeping up with industry advancements and industry standards.
  • Conduct market research to determine what your key demographic demands.
  • Collaborate with other departments to produce a prototype.

Requirements:

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering or Game Design.
  • At least 2-3 years of relevant experience is required.
  • Enthusiasm and knowledge in the game and tech industry.
  • Knowledge of programming languages such as Python and C++ is a plus.
  • Portfolio of projects on Behance should be included in your CV.

Tester

We're hunting for a game tester that can evaluate and examine our games before they're launched. You will be expected to play every game to completion in this job, identifying any bugs or anomalies along the way. You'll also have to look for hidden things, and suggest new features or improvements. You should have an in-depth understanding of video game design, a passion for gaming, and a sense of balance to succeed as a computer game tester. You need to be aware of a gamer's psychology and take note of any factors that might affect the product's sales performance.

Responsibilities:

  • Locating and investigating areas that the game's designers did not expect.
  • Playing with different characters to check.
  • Giving the game a rating in terms of entertainment and complexity.
  • Alerting the game's designers of any problems.
  • Recommending game features or modifications.

Requirements:

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or related field.
  • Computer game testing experience is required.
  • PC, console, and mobile gaming experience is preferred.
  • Pay attention to detail.
  • Excellent communication abilities.
  • Patience and perseverance are important.
